# Objection handling

Copy lands better when it anticipates the reader's doubts. Use the target ICP's known objections
(from the ICP definition) to pre-empt resistance with proof — without sounding defensive.

## Method

- For each relevant objection of the target ICP, address it *indirectly*: state the reassurance and the evidence, not the objection verbatim.
- Show, don't tell: a concrete, evidence-backed capability beats an adjective.
- Match the objection to the channel — a sales one-pager or battlecard may address objections head-on; a blog or social post should weave the reassurance into the narrative.

## Reframing examples

- "Is this just another scanner I'll run once?" → lead with the continuous, ongoing value, not a one-time result.
- "We have an internal team — why a third party?" → reassure with breadth/coverage one team can't maintain.
- "What's the ROI vs. waiting?" → frame the cost of the failure mode being prevented.

## Guardrails

- Only use objections and reassurances backed by approved messaging or evidence — never fabricate a counter-claim.
- Don't render objections as a Q&A unless the format (battlecard/one-pager) calls for it; usually fold the reassurance into the value story.
- Never disparage a named competitor — address the concern, not the competitor.
